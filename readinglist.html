Recommended Reading

Mostly Sci-fi and Fantasy

I like books.

I read a lot of books. I own a lot of books. I love a lot of books.

You've found my non-formal list of novels and series that I believe deserve to be read. I tend to put books I've read more recently at the top of the list, but otherwise there is no particular order. I don't rank them, as they are all of comparable quality, though you might see some reviews that are more glowing than others.


Dune

Frank Herbert

Dune isn't my favorite novel, but it's the one I always recommend first when people ask me what they should read. Dune is powerful in a way that you can't quite understand until you've read it. Historically, this one one of the first novels that showed the world that Sci-Fi could be epic in the same way that fantasy had previously monopolized.

There are few instances of poetry, in fiction or otherwise, that possess gravity comparable to The Litany Against Fear.

The Discworld Series

Terry Prachett

Absolutely my favorite series ever. Over fourty novels, all written with humor, thoughtfulness and most importantly incredible skill. Characters in the Discworld are beyond well developed, with some appearing in connected adventures across over a dozen novels, each occasionally referencing, hearing about or encountering individuals that star in their own stories. The Discworld is at once incredibly ambitious in scope, yet self-contained in each specific book. Though I recommend reading them in the order they were written, it is entirely possible to simply pick them up at random.
